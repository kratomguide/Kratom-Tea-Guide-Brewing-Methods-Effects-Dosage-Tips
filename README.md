## What Is Kratom Tea and How Does It Work?

Kratom tea is a hot water extraction of alkaloids from dried leaves of Mitragyna speciosa, a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. The brewing process pulls mitragynine and other compounds into solution, creating a traditional preparation method that advocates consider the safest consumption approach[3].

**The mechanism:** Mitragynine hits opioid receptors but doesn't slow breathing like morphine does at lower doses. The tea contains two primary active compounds:

- **Mitragynine** - the dominant alkaloid responsible for most effects
- **7-hydroxymitragynine (7-OH)** - makes up less than 2% of natural leaf content but is approximately 13 times more potent than morphine[3]

Pharmacologically, these alkaloids interact with mu-opioid receptors in the brain and body. The science here shows partial agonist activity rather than full agonist effects seen with traditional opioids, which changes the risk profile but doesn't eliminate it.

**What we know about absorption:** Tea allows for gradual alkaloid absorption through the digestive system. Effects build over 10-20 minutes rather than hitting immediately, giving users more control over intake compared to concentrated extracts[3].

## How to Brew Kratom Tea: Step-by-Step Methods

Brewing kratom tea properly extracts alkaloids without degrading them through excessive heat. The traditional method remains the most studied approach.

### Basic Hot Water Steep Method

1. **Measure your dose** - Start with 2-3 grams of dried kratom powder or crushed leaf
2. **Heat water** - Bring water to 180-200째F (not boiling, which may degrade alkaloids)
3. **Steep** - Add kratom to hot water and let sit for 15-20 minutes
4. **Strain** - Filter through cheesecloth, coffee filter, or fine mesh strainer
5. **Consume** - Drink the liquid, discarding plant material

**Common mistake:** Using boiling water. Temperatures above 212째F may break down some alkaloids, reducing potency.

### Simmering Method

Some users prefer a gentle simmer for better extraction:

- Add kratom to water in a pot
- Heat to a gentle simmer (not a rolling boil)
- Maintain low heat for 10-15 minutes
- Strain and serve

**Choose this method if:** You're using whole or crushed leaves rather than powder, as longer extraction time helps pull alkaloids from larger plant material.

### Flavor Improvements

Kratom tea tastes bitter and earthy. Research-minded users often add:

- Lemon or lime juice (citric acid may aid alkaloid extraction)
- Honey or sweetener
- Ginger tea
- Mint leaves

**The evidence:** Limited research exists on whether acidic additions improve bioavailability, but anecdotal reports suggest citrus may help.

## Documented Risks and Safety Concerns

The FDA has concluded kratom is not appropriate for use as a dietary supplement due to liver toxicity, seizures, and potential for substance use disorder[3]. Clinical data from recent years shows escalating safety concerns.

### Overdose Deaths and Mortality Data

More than 2,000 overdose deaths since 2021 involved kratom, though most cases included multiple substances[3]. Santa Barbara County confirmed two overdose deaths in 2025 and five over the past three years[6].

**What we know:** Kratom rarely appears as the sole cause of death in toxicology reports. According to research, polysubstance use complicates determining kratom's specific contribution to mortality.

**Study limitations:** Death certificates and toxicology reports don't always test for kratom alkaloids, potentially underestimating involvement in fatal overdoses.

### Specific Health Risks

**Liver toxicity:** Case reports document acute liver injury associated with kratom use, ranging from mild enzyme elevation to fulminant hepatic failure requiring transplantation.

**Seizures:** Multiple case series report seizure activity in kratom users, though the mechanism remains unclear. Receptors respond to high alkaloid concentrations in ways that may lower seizure threshold.

**Respiratory depression:** While mitragynine shows less respiratory depression than traditional opioids at equivalent analgesic doses, high doses or combinations with other substances increase this risk[6].

**Withdrawal symptoms:** Prolonged use leads to physical dependence. Users report withdrawal symptoms including muscle aches, insomnia, irritability, and cravings after cessation[6].

### Substance Use Disorder Potential

Research shows kratom activates reward pathways in the brain. The evidence suggests dependence develops with regular use, though addiction liability compared to traditional opioids remains debated.

**Choose extreme caution if:** You have a history of substance use disorder, take medications metabolized by liver enzymes, or have pre-existing liver conditions.

## Who Should Avoid Kratom Tea

Safety data indicates certain populations face elevated risks from kratom consumption.

**Absolute contraindications (do not use):**

- Pregnant or breastfeeding women (no safety data exists)
- Individuals with liver disease or elevated liver enzymes
- People with seizure disorders
- Anyone taking medications metabolized by CYP3A4 or CYP2D6 enzymes
- Individuals with history of substance use disorder

**Relative contraindications (extreme caution):**

- People taking antidepressants, benzodiazepines, or other CNS depressants
- Individuals with cardiovascular conditions
- Those operating heavy machinery or driving
- Anyone under 18 years old

**Drug interactions:** Kratom alkaloids inhibit certain liver enzymes. Pharmacologically, this means kratom can increase blood levels of medications processed through these pathways, including some statins, blood thinners, and psychiatric medications.

**Research gaps:** Clinical data on drug interactions remains extremely limited. Most knowledge comes from case reports rather than controlled studies.

Yes, though subsequent brews contain fewer alkaloids. The first steep extracts most water-soluble compounds. A second steep may produce milder effects but won't match the initial brew's potency.

Standard employment drug panels don't test for kratom alkaloids. Specialized tests can detect mitragynine and its metabolites, but these aren't routine. Some organizations specifically test for kratom, particularly in treatment programs or probation settings.

Mitragynine has a half-life of approximately 24 hours. Complete elimination takes roughly 5-6 days for most users, though detection windows vary based on dose, frequency, and individual metabolism.

**How often can you safely drink kratom tea?**
